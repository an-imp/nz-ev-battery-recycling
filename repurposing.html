Overview

When an electric vehicle (EV) battery reaches the end of its useful life for driving, it doesn’t necessarily become waste. Most EV batteries still retain around 70–80 percent of their original capacity, making them ideal candidates for second-life applications.

Repurposing extends the battery's lifespan, turning what would otherwise be discarded into a valuable resource that supports New Zealand’s renewable-energy and climate goals.

This stage forms a crucial bridge in the circular battery economy, maximising environmental and economic benefits while deferring the need for recycling until the battery is truly depleted.

The Concept of Second Life

Second-life use means giving retired EV batteries a new role outside the vehicle. Once tested and deemed safe, these batteries can store energy for homes, businesses, and even entire communities.

Rather than sending batteries straight to recycling plants, they are reconfigured, tested, and deployed in stationary systems that make full use of their remaining capacity.

02
Grid Support and Load Balancing

Second-life battery banks can provide peak-shaving and frequency-regulation services, helping to stabilise New Zealand’s electricity grid during high-demand periods.

03
Emergency Backup Systems

Reused batteries can act as off-grid backup power during natural disasters, supporting emergency services, hospitals, or community shelters — particularly valuable for a country prone to storms and earthquakes.

04
Renewable Integration

As Aotearoa increases its investment in wind and solar, second-life batteries can smooth fluctuations in renewable generation and enhance the reliability of clean-energy supply.

Repurposing gives EV batteries a productive second life — turning potential waste into an energy asset for Aotearoa.
