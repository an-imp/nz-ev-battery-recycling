Overview

Safety is one of the most critical aspects of electric vehicle (EV) battery recycling. Lithium-ion batteries store large amounts of energy and contain reactive materials that can become hazardous if damaged, improperly stored, or handled without care. Managing safety and fire risk is therefore central to building a responsible and resilient recycling framework for New Zealand.

Understanding the Risks

EV batteries can pose several risks during storage, transport, and recycling. These hazards highlight the importance of trained personnel, appropriate facilities, and compliance with national and international safety standards.

01
Thermal runaway

a chain reaction that can lead to overheating, fire, or explosion.

02
Short circuits

caused by physical damage, moisture exposure, or improper dismantling.

03
Toxic gas release

from electrolyte leakage or combustion.

04
Re-ignition

even after being extinguished, damaged cells may reignite due to residual charge.

Safe practices protect people today, and enable a sustainable energy future for tomorrow.

Effective EV battery recycling is only possible with robust safety systems.

By combining regulatory compliance, workforce training, fire prevention technology, and public awareness, New Zealand can:

  • Prevent fires and hazardous incidents.
  • Protect workers, communities, and the environment.
  • Build national confidence in the circular battery economy.
