Overview

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is reshaping how electric vehicle (EV) batteries are reused, repurposed, and recycled.

From automated disassembly to smart diagnostics and material recovery, AI technologies are helping to make the recycling process safer, faster, and more sustainable.

For New Zealand, where the EV recycling industry is still developing, AI represents a powerful opportunity to build intelligence into every stage of the circular battery lifecycle, ensuring maximum resource recovery with minimal environmental risk.

Smarter Battery Assessment

One of the most valuable applications of AI in the recycling process is battery health assessment.

AI systems can analyse data from the Battery Management System (BMS) to determine a packâ€™s State of Health (SOH), performance history, and safety profile.

Machine learning algorithms detect subtle patterns in voltage, temperature, and resistance that indicate whether a battery should be reused, repurposed, or recycled.

Automation and Robotic Disassembly

AI works hand-in-hand with robotics and automation to dismantle used batteries efficiently and safely. Computer-vision systems powered by AI can recognise different battery types, identify fastening points, and guide robotic arms during disassembly.

This reduces human exposure to hazardous materials, eliminates repetitive manual labour, and improves precision in separating cells, casings, and modules.

In future recycling plants, AI-driven robots could autonomously adjust their techniques for varying battery chemistries, improving throughput and recovery rates.
