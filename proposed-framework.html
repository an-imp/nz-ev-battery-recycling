Framework for sustainable electric vehicle battery recycling in New Zealand

The proposed framework provides a comprehensive and collaborative model for managing the entire lifecycle of electric vehicle (EV) batteries in Aotearoa New Zealand. It is designed to support the country’s transition toward a low-emission, low-waste, and circular economy, as envisioned in Rautaki Pāra – Waste Strategy (2023).

The framework ensures that EV batteries are safely collected, reused, repurposed, and recycled, keeping valuable materials in circulation for as long as possible while protecting people in New Zealand.

Circular Lifecycle Model

The framework is built around a circular battery economy, which replaces the traditional linear model of "make–use–dispose".

Under this approach:

  • Reuse: Batteries retaining 70–80 % capacity are refurbished for continued vehicle use or lighter electric transport.
  • Repurpose: Batteries with around 50–60 % capacity are redeployed in second-life applications such as home solar storage or community energy backup.
  • Recycle: Batteries below 50 % capacity are processed using hydrometallurgical or direct-recycling technologies to recover valuable metals like lithium, cobalt, nickel, and copper


Each pathway is supported by strong assessment, testing, and data-tracking systems to ensure safety, quality, and traceability.

Stakeholder Roles

The success of this framework depends on coordinated action among key stakeholders, each contributing to the circular ecosystem.

  • Vehicle Owners Responsible for maintaining battery safety and returning used units to approved collection or recycling points.
  • Second-Life Providers Organisations that repurpose batteries for stationary storage or industrial use under strict quality standards.
  • Government Agencies Oversee policy, regulation, and funding through mechanisms like the Waste Minimisation Fund.


Together, these entities form an integrated network ensuring that every EV battery is responsibly managed from production to end-of-life.

Ministry for the Environment (MfE)

Sets national targets and oversees waste-strategy implementation.

Waka Kotahi / NZ Transport Agency

Enforces end-of-life collection and transport standards.

New Zealand Customs Service

Ensures imports and exports comply with hazardous-goods regulations.

Policy and Circular Economy Alignment

The framework aligns with national and global sustainability objectives by integrating the waste hierarchy (Reduce → Reuse → Recycle → Recover → Dispose).
It calls for government-led stewardship programs to fund collection networks, promote public awareness, and create incentives for reuse and recycling initiatives.
By embedding these principles, the framework reduces landfill pressure, mitigates fire and chemical risks, and supports a resilient low-carbon economy.

Vision for Aotearoa New Zealand

The proposed framework envisions a connected, transparent, and self-sustaining EV battery ecosystem, where every battery is tracked, tested, and transformed into new value. Through cross-sector collaboration and continuous innovation, New Zealand can build a model that is not only environmentally sound but also economically viable and socially responsible.
